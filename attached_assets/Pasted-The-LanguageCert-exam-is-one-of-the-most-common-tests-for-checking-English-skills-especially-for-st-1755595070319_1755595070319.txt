Exam Pattern for Language Cert Test
The Language Cert is easier compared to other English exams, and its pattern is very simple. The test checks four main skills:

Reading
Listening
Writing
Speaking
Unlike other language tests, Language Cert allows candidates to take the exam in two parts. The Writing, Reading, and Listening sections are taken together, while the Speaking section can be taken separately at a later time.

Each section is scored individually:

Listening: 15 marks
Reading: 20 marks
Writing: 6 marks
Language Cert Speaking: 16 marks

What is Exam Session in Language Cert?
In the Language Cert English test, an exam session means the time when a student takes one part of the test. This can be either the written test (which includes Reading, Listening, and Writing) or the speaking test (where the student talks with an examiner).

Moreover, these sections can be taken together or separately, depending on the exam type. The Language Cert exam in Pakistan is easier and shorter than IELTS or TOEFL. The written test takes a maximum of 2.5 hours, while the speaking test lasts only fifteen minutes.
