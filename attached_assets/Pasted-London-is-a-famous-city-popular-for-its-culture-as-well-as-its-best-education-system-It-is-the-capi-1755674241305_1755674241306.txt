List of the London Top Universities
London is not the cheapest place for students. There are numerous universities to select from. It is good to check out the top universities in London for international students. Let’s have a look at the best London universities list below.

University College London
It was established in 1826 and was the first best university in London. It is located in Bloomsbury in the West End of London, which is a popular area known for theatres, tourist sites, and government buildings. 

UCL is also called London’s global university because students from more than 150 countries study there, and it has connections with three-hundred universities throughout the world. UCL has twenty-nine Nobel Prize winners among its past students, including Otto Hahn, who worked on radioactivity.

Imperial College London
Imperial College London is one of the best universities in London. It is part of a group of six top schools, including Oxford and Cambridge. It is located in South Kensington in central London.

The university was established in 1907 by combining three colleges. Imperial is the only university in the United Kingdom that focuses only on science, engineering, business, and medicine. The university gives out more than ninety-five-hundred degrees every year and has a strong worldwide ranking.

London School of Economics and Political Science
The London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE) has been a leader in social sciences since 1895. It is a major place for academic discussions and hosts lectures by important politicians.

LSE provides thirty-six undergraduate degrees and more than 140 postgraduate and doctorate degrees across twenty-five departments. This best university in London covers a variety of social science subjects, including anthropology and finance.

King’s College London
King’s College London was made in 1829 by King George IV and the Duke of Wellington. It is one of the most respected universities in London and around the world.

The main campuses are located on the Strand in central London. It is close to famous places like the Houses of Parliament, Waterloo Bridge, and Guy’s Hospital. It has a student community of 28,000 people from all different backgrounds.

Queen Mary, University of London
Queen Mary, University of London has a history since 1785. It was made by combining four medical colleges: London School of Medicine and Dentistry, Westfield College, The Royal Hospital of St Bartholomew, and Queen Mary College.

The main campus of this university is in Mile End, East London. It provides affordable student housing and is close to popular places like Brick Lane and Spitalfields Market. Queen Mary also provides a variety of courses and is part of the world’s largest academic health science center.

City, University of London
City, University of London is included in the top London universities name that started in 1894. With nearly 20,000 students and over 220,000 graduates from 160 countries, City is part of the University of London.

 Located in central London, City focuses on business and professional studies. It provides many undergraduate and postgraduate courses across six different schools. The university’s research is highly rated, with 86% of it considered the best.

Royal Holloway University of London
Royal Holloway, University of London (RHUL) is another name on the list of London universities and has a modern campus on 135 acres of land in Egham, Surrey, which is 19 miles from central London. The university has six schools and 21 departments, with around 11,000 students from 100 countries.

It is recognized as one of the 30 most selective universities in the UK by The Sutton Trust. RHUL provides a high-quality education and has great facilities, including a £57 million library and student services center.

Brunel University London
Brunel University London was established in 1966 and is a lively university located in Uxbridge, West London. It has nearly 13,000 students from over 100 countries. Brunel focuses on teaching, research, and encouraging new ideas.

It is just 45 minutes away from central London, making it easy to travel there.  Since it’s a campus-based university, students have easy access to many facilities, making their academic experience more connected and enjoyable.

SOAS University of London
SOAS University of London is located in Bloomsbury and is famous for its focus on studying Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. It was created in 1916, it is strong in subjects like African and Asian law, commercial law, environmental law, languages, and human rights.

 The central location of this London best university gives students easy access to many resources, cultural sites, and plenty of internship and job opportunities in this busy and growing city.

Birkbeck, University of London
Birkbeck, University of London was made in 1823 and has been part of the University of London ranking since 1920. It is popular for providing evening classes, making it easier for students to work and study at the same time.

With more than 90% of its teachers involved in research, Birkbeck provides courses in subjects like humanities, social sciences, business, law, and science. Located in Bloomsbury, central London, the university provides many chances for great career growth.
