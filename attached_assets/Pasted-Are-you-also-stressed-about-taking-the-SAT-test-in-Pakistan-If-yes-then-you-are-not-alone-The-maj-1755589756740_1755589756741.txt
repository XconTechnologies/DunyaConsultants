What is SAT Test in Pakistan?

You might have wondered what SAT stands for in Pakistan, right? The Scholastic Aptitude Test, or SAT, is a standardized test commonly used by educational institutions or colleges to assess students’ academic skills. The SAT assessment test is conducted by the College Board, USA (an American non-profit organization) in Pakistan.

Moreover, the SAT is a multiple-choice exam and gives students a fixed time to complete it. Students are evaluated in two main subject areas: Math, Reading, and Writing. Every year, around three million students take part in SAT exam registration. It is taken approximately 7 times each year.

Format of SAT Test in Pakistan
The duration of the SAT in Pakistan is around two hours and fourteen minutes. It has two main sections. Reading, Writing, and Math.

You will have sixty-four minutes to complete the writing and reading sections, and the math section will take around seventy minutes to complete.
Every section of the test has two modules of the same length, separated by a 10 min break.
Furthermore, these two modules vary depending on the complexity level. The first one consists of easy to medium questions, while the second one has hard questions.
Note that the difficulty level of the 2nd module is based on your test performance in the 1st module.
In the Math section, a few questions may require you to write down your answers.
The best thing about the test is that there is no penalty for wrong answers, so don’t leave any questions blank.
SAT Exam Fee in Pakistan

SAT Late Registration Fee
The SAT late registration fee for international students is around thirty-four dollars. You will have to pay this fee if you register after the registration deadline.

Test Center Fee
There is a test center fee of around twenty-four dollars, but this fee is only for selective locations. The list of test centers that take this fee can help you determine which centers charge it.

Change Center Fee
Those who want to change their center will have to pay an additional twenty-nine dollars.

Late Cancel Fee
If you want to cancel your SAT exam registration after the change deadline, you will have to pay a Late cancel fee of around thirty-nine dollars.

Cancel Registration Fee
To cancel your registration by the change deadline, you will need to pay a cancel registration fee of around twenty-nine dollars.

How do you decrease the SAT Registration Fee in Pakistan?
To lower registration costs, SAT Fee Waivers are offered to 11th and 12th-grade students and those who need financial help. However, these fee waivers are not for Pakistani students, and they will have to pay the registration fee.

In addition, international students living in the USA or its territories and US citizens living overseas are qualified for SAT Fee Waivers.
